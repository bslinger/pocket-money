Name it clearly
Use language your kids understand. Kids see the same name so clarity matters.
②
Set what it's worth
Works with any currency. You can use different amounts for different kids on the same chore.
③④
Choose how often and which days
Daily, weekly, monthly, or one-off. For daily/weekly chores pick specific days. Quiddo resets it automatically each cycle.
⑤
Assign to one or more kids
A chore can belong to one child, rotate between kids, or be "up for grabs" — first to claim it gets the credit.
Pro tip: approval toggle
Turn off approval for obvious chores ("made my bed") to reduce workload. Keep it on for bigger tasks where you want to check before paying out.
Chore types
Three ways to structure chores
🔁
Recurring
Assigned to a specific child, repeating on your schedule. Appears automatically each cycle.
Examples
• Dishwasher — Olivia — Daily
• Take out bins — Noah — Weekly
• Clean bedroom — Mia — Sat
🎯
Up for grabs
Posted for any assigned kid to claim. First to tap "Done" earns it. Great for sibling competition.
Examples
• Walk the dog — Olivia or Liam
• Water the garden — Any kid
• Fold laundry — Liam or Noah
⚡
One-off bonus
A spontaneous task posted on the spot. Doesn't repeat. Great for extra jobs or unexpected situations.
Examples
• Help unpack shopping
• Wash the car (school hols)
• Help set up for a party
